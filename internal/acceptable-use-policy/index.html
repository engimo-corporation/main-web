ENGIMO Internal

Acceptable Use Policy

Version: 2026.1 Effective: March 25, 2026 Scope: All Personnel

1. Introduction

This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) outlines the guidelines and expectations for the use of ENGIMO Corporation’s internal services, devices, and digital assets. Our goal is to protect our employees, partners, and the company from illegal or damaging actions while ensuring a productive work environment.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and temporary workers. It covers all resources including:

  • Microsoft 365 Business environment (Outlook, Teams, OneDrive, SharePoint).
  • Windows-based workstations and corporate mobile devices.
  • Internal network infrastructure and VPN access.

3. User Responsibilities & Security

All users must have Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) enabled for their Microsoft 365 accounts at all times.
  • Authentication: Never share passwords. Use corporate-approved credential managers.
  • Device Safety: Windows devices must be locked (Win + L) when unattended.
  • Updates: Do not defer critical Windows or Microsoft 365 security updates.

4. Data Protection & Privacy

All data created or stored on ENGIMO systems remains the property of the corporation. Users should have no expectation of privacy regarding company-provided devices or cloud services.

  • Store sensitive documents in designated SharePoint libraries.
  • Use "Specific People" link settings for external sharing in OneDrive.
  • Report suspected phishing attempts immediately via the Outlook "Report" button.

5. Prohibited Activities

Unauthorized Access

Attempting to bypass security controls or access data without permission.

Illegal Content

Transmitting or storing material that violates local or federal laws.

Harassment

Using Teams or Email for derogatory or defamatory communications.

Data Exfiltration

Moving corporate data to personal cloud storage or unencrypted drives.

Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action up to termination or legal action.
